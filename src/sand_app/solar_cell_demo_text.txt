We fabricated single-junction perovskite solar cells in the inverted (p-i-n)
  architecture on ITO-coated glass substrates. The substrates were cleaned by
  sequential sonication in detergent, deionized water, acetone, and isopropanol for
  15 minutes each, then treated with UV-ozone for 15 minutes. The hole-selective
  self-assembled monolayer MeO-2PACz was spin coated from a 1 mM solution in ethanol
  at 3000 rpm for 30 s and annealed at 100 C for 10 min. The formamidinium-rich
  absorber, Cs0.05FA0.95PbI3, was deposited from a 1.5 M precursor solution in a
  DMF:DMSO 4:1 mixture by spin coating at 4000 rpm for 35 s; 150 uL of chlorobenzene
  antisolvent was dropped 10 s before the end of the spin program. The film was then
  annealed at 120 C for 30 min. A 20 nm C60 layer and an 8 nm BCP layer were
  thermally evaporated as the electron-selective contact, followed by a 100 nm silver
  electrode. This champion device had an active area of 0.16 cm2 and a bandgap of
  1.53 eV, reaching a power conversion efficiency of 23.4% with an open-circuit
  voltage of 1.16 V, a short-circuit current density of 24.8 mA/cm2, and a fill
  factor of 81.3% under AM1.5G illumination. It retained 92% of its initial
  efficiency after 1000 h of maximum-power-point tracking under continuous
  illumination.

  For comparison, we also processed a conventional (n-i-p) reference cell based on
  MAPbI3 on an FTO/compact-TiO2/mesoporous-TiO2 substrate. The MAPbI3 layer was spin
  coated from a 1.2 M solution in DMF and annealed at 100 C for 10 min, and
  Spiro-OMeTAD was used as the hole-transport layer with a thermally evaporated 80 nm
  gold electrode. This device showed a bandgap of 1.60 eV, a power conversion
  efficiency of 18.2%, an open-circuit voltage of 1.08 V, a short-circuit current
  density of 22.1 mA/cm2, and a fill factor of 76.4%, measured over an active area of
  0.09 cm2 under AM1.5G illumination.
